Knowable – Trustpilot Review Analysis Platform

Bachelor project · NLP · R · Web scraping · Data visualisation

Knowable challenges the idea that a simple 1–5 star rating on Trustpilot can fully represent customer experience. By analysing the written reviews behind the stars, the project explores how text analytics can support both companies and consumers in making more informed decisions.

R NLP Sentiment analysis Topic modelling Web scraping Shiny

Context & challenge

Online reviews are central to how people evaluate companies. However, star ratings compress complex experiences into a single number. This project set out to explore: What do customers actually say in their reviews – and how does this align with the visible rating?

The goal was to build a data pipeline and prototype that can extract, analyse and present review content in a way that reveals patterns, themes and sentiments that are otherwise hidden behind the star score.

My role

  • Defined the overall concept and research question.
  • Developed the full data pipeline in R – from web scraping to analysis.
  • Implemented sentiment and topic modelling on Trustpilot reviews.
  • Designed and implemented a Shiny prototype to present insights.
  • Interpreted findings and wrote the analytical and methodological parts of the report.

Process & methods

Data collection

I implemented a custom web scraping function in R to retrieve structured data from selected company pages on Trustpilot. The scraper collected rating, text, date and basic metadata for each review.

Text processing & analysis

The reviews were cleaned and tokenised to prepare them for analysis. I applied:

  • Sentiment analysis to understand the emotional tone across reviews.
  • Topic modelling to detect recurring themes in what customers write about.
  • Basic frequency and co-occurrence analysis to see which terms cluster together.

Interface prototype

In Shiny, I created interactive views that allow users to:

  • Filter reviews by company and rating.
  • Explore topics and sentiment distributions.
  • See representative reviews for each topic/theme.

Outcome & impact

The resulting concept, Knowable, shows how companies and consumers can move beyond star scores and engage with the language behind them. For companies, the prototype suggests a way to:

  • Identify recurring complaints or strengths in their service.
  • Compare themselves to competitors on more than just ratings.
  • Track how sentiment changes over time.

For consumers, it demonstrates how review content can help calibrate expectations: a “3-star” for one person might describe a good experience for another.

Reflections & learnings

Working end-to-end with scraping, cleaning, modelling and interface design strengthened my ability to bridge technical work and user-facing insight. It also highlighted how data quality, sampling and modelling assumptions shape the stories we tell with data.
