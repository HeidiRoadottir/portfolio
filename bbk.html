RPA in Barnabókaklubbin – Automation in a Children’s Book Club

3rd semester Master project · Digital transformation · RPA · Case study

Barnabókaklubbin is a Faroese children’s book club whose everyday work relies on manual coordination across loosely connected systems. In this project, we explored how Robotic Process Automation (RPA) can support membership onboarding while still leaving room for human judgement, local knowledge and organisational learning.

Case study RPA Power Automate Desktop Service blueprinting Qualitative interviews Digital transformation

Context

Membership administration in Barnabókaklubbin happens across a web form, Outlook, Excel, Navision 2018 and Brevo (CRM). None of these systems are fully integrated, so staff move data manually between them and use their own overview and judgement to keep everything consistent and running. Membership creation for a single child can take around ten minutes and depends heavily on one key employee knowing the “right way” to do things.

This makes the organisation vulnerable: if the person with the overview is sick or leaves, it becomes difficult to keep track of members, payments and book shipments – but a full system replacement is neither realistic nor affordable in the short term.

Research focus

The project investigates how RPA can support membership onboarding in a small, low-maturity organisation without replacing human judgement. We focus on the membership registration flow because it is well-defined, repetitive and based on structured input, yet time-consuming and error-prone when done manually.

Methodologically, we combine qualitative interviews, process mapping and service blueprinting with a design-based approach: we analyse current practice, derive design principles and implement a working RPA prototype that can be evaluated in the organisation.

My role – technical lead

I was responsible for the entire technical side of the project:

  • Designing the overall automation concept and flow structure.
  • Developing the full Power Automate Desktop flow for membership onboarding.
  • Implementing robust parsing of HTML e-mails from the online registration form into structured variables.
  • Automating data entry in Navision 2018, including debtor creation, payment setup and the link to Rokningardepilin.
  • Automating creation of webshop users and Brevo CRM contacts connected to the new member.
  • Debugging selectors, handling timing issues and making the flow modular and maintainable.
  • Documenting the flow in detail and preparing material for testing and evaluation.

Process & approach

1. Understanding existing work

Together with my group, I mapped the current (“as-is”) membership process using interviews and a service blueprint. This showed how much of the daily work consists of “glue tasks” – copying data between systems, checking for errors and compensating for missing integration – and how strongly the process relies on one person’s tacit knowledge.

2. From analysis to design principles

Based on the analysis, we formulated design principles for the future solution: focus on stable, rule-based steps; keep the automation modular; make data quality visible; and ensure a clear division of labour between RPA and staff. Automation should support the existing practice where it is already stable, not introduce completely new ways of working overnight.

3. Building the RPA flow

I implemented a Power Automate Desktop flow split into four logical modules: Main (parsing the e-mail), Navision2 (debtor creation and payment setup), Webshop users (creating the webshop login) and Brevo (creating the CRM contact). Each module can be adapted or extended separately if systems change.

What the automation does

In practice, the flow works like this:

  • Reads the newest registration e-mail from a dedicated inbox via IMAP and cleans the HTML into line-based text.
  • Maps field labels (e.g. child’s name, guardian, address, bank details) to variables using a custom “previous label” strategy, including date conversion and basic validation.
  • Opens Navision 2018, creates a new debtor, fills in master data and financial settings, and updates Rokningardepilin with account and registration numbers.
  • Creates a linked webshop user with the correct customer number and default login settings.
  • Opens Brevo in the browser, navigates to the CRM module and creates a contact subscribed to the relevant lists.

The only manual step for staff is to trigger the flow from a desktop shortcut and provide the shared passwords when prompted.

Evaluation & impact

We evaluated the prototype through a test membership registration together with the responsible employee. The flow completed the entire onboarding – across e-mail, Navision, webshop and Brevo – in about four minutes. The equivalent manual process normally takes around ten minutes and requires much more active concentration, system-switching and double-checking.

Even though we only ran a limited test, it showed that the flow is technically robust in a standard case and that it aligns well with the staff member’s expectations. At the same time, the test highlighted that the solution will require ongoing maintenance when input formats, systems or business rules change.

Key learnings

  • In small organisations, selective automation of stable sub-processes is often more realistic than “full automation” of everything.
  • Data quality work and clear process descriptions are necessary pre-conditions for RPA – otherwise the robot mainly automates existing mess.
  • Modular flow design makes it easier to debug and evolve the solution when the system landscape gradually changes.
  • Human judgement remains central: the RPA flow handles the repetitive, rule-based work, while staff keep responsibility for exceptions, edge cases and member communication.

Overall, the project shows how Power Automate can act as a practical bridge between Barnabókaklubbin’s current systems and a more integrated digital infrastructure in the future.
